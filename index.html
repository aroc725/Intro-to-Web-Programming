Apple Computers - From the Apple I to the Macintosh

Welcome to my website about Apple computers.

Introduction to Apple's Computer History

Apple has been selling computers since its founding by Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak in 1976. The company revolutionized personal computing with innovative designs and user-friendly interfaces.

The first computer Apple ever sold was the Apple I, which was sold as a kit for electronics enthusiasts and hobbyists.

Major Computers Sold by Apple

  • The Apple I - The first computer kit that started it all
  • The Apple II - The breakthrough personal computer
  • The Apple III - An ambitious but troubled business computer
  • The Lisa - Pioneer of graphical user interfaces
  • The Macintosh - The computer "for the rest of us"

Historical Computer Statistics

Sales data and specifications for major Apple computers (1976-1984)
Computer Name Release Year Operating System Estimated Units Sold
Apple I 1976 Integer BASIC (in ROM), Monitor (system monitor program) Approximately 200 units (mostly kit form)
Apple II 1977 Integer BASIC (in ROM), later Apple DOS 3.1/3.2 Approximately 5-6 million units (entire Apple II series, 1977-1993)
Apple III 1980 Apple SOS (Sophisticated Operating System) 1.0 65,000 to 120,000 units total (considered a commercial failure)
Macintosh (128K) 1984 System 1.0 (Finder 1.0) 70,000 units sold by May 1984 (successful launch)
