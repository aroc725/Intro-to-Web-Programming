The Founders of Apple

In the Beginning...

Apple was founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. This trio of visionaries would change the world of personal computing forever, though their partnership would not last long.

However, Wayne left the company just two weeks later, selling his share back to the two Steves for a mere $800 - a decision that would cost him billions in today's money.

Meet the Founders

Steve Jobs (1955-2011)

Steve Jobs was a visionary entrepreneur who co-founded Apple Inc. Known for his perfectionist approach and revolutionary product presentations, Jobs transformed Apple from a garage startup into one of the world's most valuable companies. His focus on design, user experience, and innovation led to iconic products like the iPhone, iPad, and MacBook.

Steve Wozniak (Born 1950)

Steve Wozniak, affectionately known as "Woz," was the engineering genius behind the early Apple computers. A brilliant programmer and hardware designer, Wozniak single-handedly created the Apple I and Apple II computers. His technical innovations laid the foundation for the personal computer revolution and made computing accessible to everyday users.

Ronald Wayne (Born 1934)

Ronald Wayne was the third co-founder of Apple, serving as the company's first chairman and holding a 10% stake. As the oldest of the trio at 41, Wayne was brought in to provide adult supervision and mediate between the two Steves. However, concerned about the financial risks and potential liabilities, he sold his stake back to Jobs and Wozniak for $800 just 12 days after the company's founding. His 10% share would be worth over $100 billion today.

Quick Facts About Apple's Founding

  • Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak met through their mutual friend Bill Fernandez in 1971, bonding over their shared love of electronics and pranks.
  • Apple Computer Company was officially founded on April 1, 1976, in the famous garage at 2066 Crist Drive in Los Altos, California.
  • Ronald Wayne sold his 10% shares for $800, later receiving an additional $1,500 payout. This stake would be worth over $100 billion today.
  • Wozniak invented the revolutionary Apple I computer in that garage, designing both the hardware and software that would launch the personal computer era.
